Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How many SVG files can I convert at once?
You can convert up to 100 SVG files simultaneously, with a total size limit of 50MB. For larger batches, you can process them in multiple sessions.
What's the difference between SVG and JPG formats?
SVG is a vector format that uses mathematical equations to create graphics, making it scalable without quality loss. JPG is a raster format that uses pixels, making it ideal for photographs but losing quality when scaled.
Will converting to JPG reduce image quality?
JPG uses lossy compression, which may cause minor quality reduction. However, our converter uses high-quality settings (90% by default) to minimize any noticeable loss while significantly reducing file size.
Are my files secure during conversion?
Absolutely! All processing happens locally in your web browser. Your files never leave your computer and are not stored on any server.
Can I adjust the quality of the JPG output?
Yes! Use the quality slider to adjust the compression level from 50% (smaller file size) to 100% (best quality). The default setting of 90% provides an excellent balance.
Do you keep copies of my converted files?
No, we don't store any of your files. The conversion happens entirely in your browser, and files are deleted from memory once you close the page.
